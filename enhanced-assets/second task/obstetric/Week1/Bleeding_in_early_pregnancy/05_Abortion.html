Definition & Etiology

Foundational concepts and the underlying causes of pregnancy loss before viability
Definition
Termination of pregnancy before viability of the fetus, i.e. before 20 weeks.
Incidence: Varies between 12–25%.

Etiology

The following are established causes of spontaneous abortion:

  • Chromosomal abnormalities — 50% of early abortions.
  • Maternal infections — Acute fever.
  • Trauma — External or during operations.
  • Immunological causes — Systemic lupus erythematosus and antiphospholipid antibodies.
  • Uterine defects — Septum, Asherman's syndrome.
  • Idiopathic — No identifiable cause.
  • Endocrine — Progesterone deficiency, hypothyroidism.
  • Drugs and environmental causes.
  • Maternal anoxia and malnutrition.
💎
Clinical Pearl: Chromosomal abnormalities account for ~50% of early abortions, making them the single most important etiology. Recurrent genetic testing of abortus tissue is therefore a key investigation in habitual abortion.

Threatened Abortion

The earliest, most benign form — pregnancy is still viable and may continue

Clinical Picture

  • Symptoms and signs of pregnancy coincide with its duration.
  • Vaginal bleeding: slight or mild, bright red in colour.
  • Pain: absent or slight.
  • Cervix: closed.
  • U/S: living fetus.

Management

  • Bed rest and avoid strenuous activity and sexual intercourse.
  • Progesterone.
  • Follow up.
📝
Note: The defining feature of threatened abortion is a closed cervix with a living fetus on ultrasound. The pregnancy is still viable and may continue normally.

Inevitable Abortion

Vaginal bleeding becomes heavy and the cervix dilates — the pregnancy cannot be saved

Clinical Picture

  • Symptoms and signs of pregnancy.
  • Heavy vaginal bleeding with clots.
  • Suprapubic colic referred to the back.
  • Dilated cervix.

Management

  • Medical: Oxytocin drip, prostaglandins.
  • Surgical: Evacuation.
  • Blood transfusion if needed.
⚠️
Warning: The combination of heavy bleeding + dilated cervix + colicky suprapubic pain referred to the back indicates the abortion is now inevitable and active management is required to prevent further blood loss.

Complete Abortion

All products of conception have been expelled and the uterus is empty

Definition

All products of conception have been expelled from the uterus.

Clinical Picture

  • Bleeding: mild and decreases.
  • Cessation of pain.
  • Closed cervix.
  • Uterus: slightly larger than normal.
  • Ultrasound: shows empty cavity.

Management

Ecbolics and antibiotics and no evacuation.

💎
Clinical Pearl: Unlike incomplete abortion, complete abortion requires no evacuation — only ecbolics (uterotonic agents) and antibiotics. Ultrasound confirmation of an empty cavity is the key diagnostic step.

Incomplete Abortion

Part of the products of conception are retained, requiring evacuation

Definition

Retention of a part of the products of conception inside the uterus.

Clinical Picture

  • Passage of a part of the conception products.
  • Bleeding is continuous.
  • Opened cervix.
  • Uterus: less than period of amenorrhea.
  • Ultrasonography: shows the retained contents.

Management

Evacuation (medical or surgical).

⚠️
Warning: The uterus is smaller than the period of amenorrhea would suggest because some products have already been passed. Continuous bleeding through an opened cervix mandates evacuation to prevent haemorrhage and sepsis.

Cervical Abortion

The products of conception are separated from the uterus but trapped in the cervical canal

Definition

The products of conception is separated from the uterus but retained inside the cervical canal.

Clinical Picture

  • Bleeding with severe lower abdominal pain referred to the back.
  • On examination, the products of conception are felt through the dilated cervix.

Management

Cervical dilatation and evacuation of cervical contents followed by curettage.

📝
Note: Cervical abortion is a clinical situation where the products of conception have separated from the uterine wall but become impacted in the cervical canal. Manual removal via cervical dilatation is required.

08

Missed Abortion

The fetus has died in utero but is retained — symptoms of pregnancy regress

Definition

Retention of dead products of conception.

Symptoms

  • Regression of pregnancy symptoms as nausea, vomiting and breast symptoms.
  • The abdomen does not increase.
  • The fetal movements are not felt.
  • A dark brown vaginal discharge may occur (prune juice discharge).

Signs

  • The uterus fails to grow and becomes firmer.
  • The cervix is closed.
  • Absent fetal heart sounds.

Investigations

  • Pregnancy test becomes negative within two weeks from the ovum death.
  • Ultrasound shows a collapsed gestational sac, absent fetal heart pulsations or fetal movement.

Treatment

Evacuation.

  • <12 weeks: Surgical evacuation is done.
  • >12 weeks: Medical evacuation (e.g. by Prostaglandins or Oxytocin).
💎
Clinical Pearl: The prune juice discharge is a classic clinical sign of missed abortion — old, dark-brown altered blood from a long-retained dead pregnancy. The uterus shrinks and firms up rather than growing.

Septic Abortion

Any type of abortion complicated by infection — a potentially life-threatening emergency

Definition

It is any type of abortion complicated by infection.

Microbiology

The most causative organisms are:

  • E. coli
  • Bacteroids
  • Anaerobic streptococci
  • Clostridia
  • Streptococci
  • Staphylococci

Clinical Picture

General Examination

  • Pyrexia, rigors and tachycardia.
  • Malaise, sweating, headache, and joint pain.
  • Jaundice.

Abdominal Examination

  • Tenderness, rigidity and distension.

Local Examination

  • Offensive vaginal discharge.
  • Tender uterus.

Treatment

  • Resuscitation
  • Prompt administration of intravenous broad-spectrum antibiotics followed by uterine evacuation.
  • Rest in semi-sitting position.
  • Observation for vital signs.
  • Oxytocin infusion: to control bleeding and enhances expulsion.
  • Evacuation of the uterus after commencing IV therapy.
  • Hysterectomy may be the last choice to safe life.
🆘
Danger — Emergency: Septic abortion is a life-threatening condition. Resuscitation must begin immediately, broad-spectrum IV antibiotics must be started before any evacuation, and hysterectomy may be required as a last resort to save the patient's life.

Recurrent (Habitual) Abortion

Three or more consecutive spontaneous abortions — requires structured workup

Definition

Three (two by some authors) or more consecutive spontaneous abortions.

Causes

(Persistent causes of abortion)

  • Genetic factors: Paternal chromosomal rearrangements, embryonic chromosomal abnormalities.
  • Anatomical factors: Septate uterus / cervical incompetence.
  • Antiphospholipid syndrome.
  • Endocrinological abnormalities: DM, thyroid abnormalities.
  • Thrombophilias.
  • Infective agents: STORCH.

Investigations

  1. Laboratory:
    • Urine analysis: for pus cell (UTI), proteins (nephritis) & glucose (DM).
    • Blood: serum creatinine, antiphospholipid antibodies and coagulation profile.
  2. U/S: To exclude submucous fibroid, incompetent cervix.
  3. HSG (Hysterosalpingography): To exclude submucous fibroid, incompetent cervix, intrauterine adhesions.
  4. Hysteroscopy: To exclude submucous fibroid, intrauterine adhesions, congenital anomalies of uterine cavity.
  5. Genetic study: of the abortus to exclude chromosomal aberrations.

Treatment

a- Before Pregnancy: Treat Detectable Causes

  • Hysteroscopic resection of uterine septum or submucous fibroid.
  • Treatment of luteal phase defect.
  • Strict control of DM.

b- During Pregnancy

  • Rest: Physical, mental (sedation) and sexual abstinence.
  • Diet: Good balanced diet.
  • Natural progesterone: if proved progesterone deficiency.
  • Others: Strict DM control, cerclage for incompetent cervix, heparin for the antiphospholipid syndrome.
💎
Clinical Pearl — STORCH Mnemonic: The classic congenital-infective causes of recurrent pregnancy loss are remembered as Syphilis · Toxoplasmosis · Other (varicella, parvovirus B19) · Rubella · Cytomegalovirus · Herpes / HIV. Always screen for these in recurrent abortion workup.
💎
Clinical Pearl — Cerclage for Cervical Incompetence: McDonald cerclage (a purse-string suture around the cervix) placed at 12–14 weeks is the standard surgical treatment for cervical incompetence in subsequent pregnancies.
