1. Diagnosis of Early Pregnancy (1st Trimester)

1.1 Symptoms

  • Secondary amenorrhea.
  • Frequency of micturition: pressure by the enlarged pregnant uterus on the urinary bladder, and pelvic congestion.
  • Nausea and vomiting: usually appears at 6 weeks and usually disappears at 12 weeks of pregnancy.
  • Breast symptoms: enlargement, mastodynia and fullness.

1.2 Signs

  • Skin: discussed before.
  • Breast: discussed before.
  • Vaginal and cervical bluish discoloration of vulva, vagina and cervix due to increased vascularity.
  • Uterine signs:
    • Size: increased.
    • Shape: Globular.
    • Consistency: Soft.
  • Hegar sign: depends on the difference in consistency between the cervix and the body of the uterus (body is softer), detected by manual examination.
  • Palmar sign: painless contractions followed by relaxation, not accompanied by cervical dilatation.

1.3 Investigations

  • Pregnancy test: depends on the detection of hCG either in urine or serum. Serum hCG is sensitive at 25 IU/mL and becomes positive one week before the missed period.
  • Ultrasonography:
    • Ring (4 weeks).
    • Sac (5 weeks).
    • Cardiac activity (6 weeks).
    • FHS — fetal heart sound (10 weeks).

2. Diagnosis of 2nd Trimester (12–28 weeks)

2.1 Symptoms

  • Amenorrhea.
  • Morning sickness and frequency of micturition decrease.
  • Abdominal enlargement.
  • Quickening: first perception of fetal movement by the pregnant woman.
    • Primigravida: 18–20 weeks.
    • Multigravida: 16–18 weeks.

2.2 Signs

  • Skin: discussed before.
  • Breast changes: as 1st trimester + 2ry areola.
  • Uterus:
    • Felt abdominally.
    • Braxton Hicks contractions: intermittent painless contractions detected by abdominal examination.
    • Ballottement (solid body moving in fluid medium) — can be:
      • External ballottement: positive after 20 weeks.
      • Internal ballottement: positive after 16 weeks.
    • Uterine soufflé: soft blowing murmur due to blood flow in the uterine arteries, synchronous with the maternal pulse.
    • Auscultation of FHS and umbilical soufflé (sharp whistling murmur due to blood flow in the umbilical vessels, synchronous with FHS) — a sure sign of pregnancy.

Clinical Pearl — Synchrony Distinguishes the Two Soufflés

Uterine soufflé is synchronous with the maternal pulse (blood flow in uterine arteries).
Umbilical soufflé is synchronous with the FHS (blood flow in umbilical vessels) and is itself a sure sign of pregnancy.

3. Diagnosis of 3rd Trimester (28–40 weeks)

3.1 Symptoms

  • The same as in the 2nd trimester, in addition to the symptoms of engagement like increased frequency of micturition and lightening.
  • Shelfing.

3.2 Signs

More evident.

  • Ultrasonography is diagnostic.

3.3 Sure Signs of Pregnancy

  • Palpation of fetal parts.
  • Palpation of fetal movement.
  • Auscultation of FHS.
  • Auscultation of umbilical soufflé.
  • Detection of fetal parts by ultrasonography.
Warning — Probable vs. Sure Signs

Many 1st-trimester findings (amenorrhea, nausea, breast changes, Hegar's sign, positive hCG) are probable signs only. A diagnosis of pregnancy becomes definitive only when one of the sure signs above is documented — direct palpation of fetal parts / movement, auscultation of FHS, or visualization by ultrasound.
