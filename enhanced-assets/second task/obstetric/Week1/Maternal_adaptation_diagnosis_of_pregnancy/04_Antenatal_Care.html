1. Definition & Objectives of Antenatal Care

Definition

Preventive obstetric program including a group of observations, medical care, investigations and advices aiming at getting pregnancy, labor and puerperium as near to normal as possible.

Objectives

  • Identification of high risk pregnancy.
  • Monitoring of high risk pregnancy.
  • Early detection of complications.
  • Giving advice for all pregnant patients.
  • Management of common minor problems.

2. Frequency of Visits

Low-Risk Group

  • Every month till 28 weeks.
  • Then every 2 weeks till 36 weeks.
  • Then every week till delivery.

High-Risk Group

  • Every 2 weeks till 28 weeks.
  • Then every week till 36 weeks.
  • Then hospitalization till delivery.

5. Screening Tests

5.1 Routine Tests

  • ABO grouping and Rh typing.
  • Urine analysis for albumin, sugar and bacterial count.
  • CBC.
  • One-hour postprandial glucose test.
  • HBsAg.
  • Rubella antibodies.
  • Cervicovaginal smear.

5.2 For Specific Populations

  • Sexually transmitted diseases (HIV, Syphilis, …etc).
  • Thalassemia (A in Asians; B in Mediterranean).
  • Sickle cell anemia (in black women).
  • Tuberculin skin test.
  • Screen for short cervix by transvaginal ultrasound.
  • Screening or diagnostic testing for trisomy 21 and other genetic disorders.
  • Screen for red blood cell antibodies and administer anti-D immune globulin to D-negative patients.

6. Antenatal Advice — Nutrition

Daily Requirements

  • Total calories: 2700 cal / day.
  • Protein: 1.5 g / kg / day (at least half should be of animal source).
  • Iron: 30–60 mg / day.
  • Calcium: 1.5 g / day.
  • Folic acid: 400 µg / day.
  • Weight gain: 10–12 kg.

7. Antenatal Advice — Lifestyle

  • Teeth care: daily cleaning by tooth paste.
  • Clothing: light clothes, avoid high-heeled shoes.
  • Bathing: avoid hot bathing and swimming pools.
  • Sexual activity: whenever there is a threat of abortion or preterm labor, coitus should be avoided. Otherwise, it is allowed with less frequency.
  • Sleep: 8 hours at night and 2 in the afternoon.
  • Travelling: avoided if there is a risk of abortion or PTL (preterm labor).
  • Special habits: avoid smoking, alcohol.

8. Immunization During Pregnancy

  • Live attenuated viral vaccines such as MMR & polio are contraindicated.
  • Inactivated viral vaccines such as influenza and rabies are safe.
Warning — Live Vaccines Contraindicated

Live attenuated vaccines (MMR, polio) are contraindicated in pregnancy. Inactivated vaccines (influenza, rabies) are safe.

10. Exposure to Irradiation

Avoided whether diagnostic or therapeutic.

  • < 5 rads: may not harm.
  • 5 – 50 rads: may harm.
  • 50 – 100 rads: CFMF (Central Nervous System MalFormation).
Clinical Pearl — Radiation Thresholds

Below 5 rads is generally considered safe; 5–50 rads is a harm-risk window; above 50 rads is associated with CFMF (CNS malformation). Note: 1 rad = 0.01 Gy.

12. Warning Symptoms of Pregnancy

  1. Any symptoms suggestive of PIH: persistent headache, blurring of vision, vomiting, and epigastric pain.
  2. Swelling of the face, fingers and limbs.
  3. Excessive watery vaginal discharge.
  4. Vaginal bleeding.
  5. Fever, dyspnea.
  6. Severe abdominal pain.
  7. Decreased fetal kicks.
Warning — Seek Immediate Care

Any of the seven symptoms above warrants urgent review. Headaches + visual changes + epigastric pain = think PIH / pre-eclampsia. Decreased fetal kicks may signal fetal compromise.
