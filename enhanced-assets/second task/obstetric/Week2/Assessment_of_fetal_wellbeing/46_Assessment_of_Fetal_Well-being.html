1ILOs & Fetal movement counting

📘 KEY DEFINITION · Intended Learning Outcome

To be able to describe various fetal well-being tests regarding the indications, procedure steps and its interpretation.

I. Fetal movement counting

Timing: After 30 weeks' gestation.

Method: The mother counts the fetal kicks in 3 hours during the period of 12 hours, from 9 a.m. to 9 p.m. for one hour at the beginning, one hour at the middle and one hour at the end of this period. The count is multiplied by 4 to get fetal kicks in 12 hours. If it is less than 10 kicks, this needs further testing with non-stress test (NST).

2Non-stress Test (NST)

📘 KEY DEFINITION · Non-stress Test (NST)

The non-stress test is used to describe fetal heart rate acceleration in response to fetal movement as a sign of fetal health typically after 32 weeks' gestation.

Method

This test involves the use of Doppler-detected fetal heart rate acceleration coincident with fetal movements perceived by the mother.

Results

Normal (reactive) NST

The presence of two or more fetal heart rate accelerations that peak at 15 beat per minute (bpm) or more above the baseline, each lasting 15 seconds or more, and all occurring within 20 minutes of beginning the test.

Abnormal NST

A fetal heart rate baseline that oscillated less than 5 beats per minute.

Frequency of testing

The interval between tests for fetal well-being, was set at 7 days. But, more frequent testing is recommended for women with post-term pregnancy, type 1 diabetes mellitus, fetal growth restriction, or maternal hypertension.

📝 Question 1 of 5
What is the primary purpose of the Non-stress Test (NST)?
The NST evaluates fetal heart rate accelerations coinciding with fetal movements. The presence of ≥2 accelerations (≥15 bpm, ≥15 seconds) within 20 minutes indicates a reactive (normal) test, reflecting an intact fetal CNS-autonomic pathway.
📝 Question 2 of 5
A reactive (normal) NST requires the presence of:
A reactive NST is defined by ≥2 accelerations of ≥15 bpm above baseline, each lasting ≥15 seconds, all occurring within 20 minutes of starting the test. This pattern reliably predicts a healthy, non-acidotic fetus.
📝 Question 3 of 5
After what gestational age is the NST typically performed?
The NST is typically performed after 32 weeks' gestation. Fetal movement counting begins at 30 weeks, and if abnormal, the NST is the next step. Before 32 weeks, the fetal autonomic nervous system may not yet produce consistent accelerations.
📝 Question 4 of 5
More frequent NST testing is recommended for which of the following conditions?
While the standard NST interval is 7 days, more frequent testing is indicated for high-risk conditions: post-term pregnancy, type 1 diabetes mellitus, fetal growth restriction, and maternal hypertension, as these increase the risk of placental insufficiency.

3Contraction Stress Test (CST)

📘 KEY DEFINITION · Contraction Stress Test (CST)

The CST evaluates fetal heart rate in response to uterine contractions induced by breast stimulation or oxytocin infusion. Once 3 contractions occur in a 10-minute period, the fetal heart rate is observed for reactivity and the occurrence of late or variable decelerations.

Interpretation

Result Definition
Negative No late or significant variable decelerations.
Positive Late decelerations following 50% or more of contractions, even if the contraction frequency is fewer than three in 10 min.
Equivocal Intermittent late decelerations or significant variable suspicious decelerations.
Equivocal Fetal heart rate decelerations that occur in the presence of hyperstimulatory contractions more frequent than every 2 min or lasting longer than 90 seconds.
Unsatisfactory Fewer than three contractions in 10 min or an uninterpretable tracing.

4Cardiotocography (CTG)

📘 KEY DEFINITION · Cardiotocography (CTG)

Cardiotocography (CTG) is a technical means of recording the fetal heart beat (cardio-) and the uterine contractions (-toco-) during pregnancy, typically in the third trimester. Simultaneous recordings are performed by two separate transducers, one for the measurement of the fetal heart rate and a second one for the uterine contractions.

Five components of CTG interpretation

  1. Uterine activity (contractions).
  2. Baseline fetal heart rate.
  3. Baseline FHR variability.
  4. Presence of accelerations.
  5. Periodic or episodic decelerations.

Uterine activity

Normal

≤ to 5 contractions in 10 minutes, averaged over a 30-minute window.

Tachysystole

>5 contractions in 10 minutes, averaged over a 30 minute window.

6Fetal Biophysical Profile (BPP)

📘 KEY DEFINITION · Fetal Biophysical Profile (BPP)

The combined use of five fetal components gives accurate method for assessing fetal health than any one used alone. Typically, these tests require 30 minutes of examiner time.

Parameter Score 2 (Normal) Score 0 (Abnormal)
Fetal heart rate reactivity Presence of reactivity, without or without presence of decelerations. Absence of reactivity.
Fetal tone — Extremity extension/flexion At least one episode of fetal extremity extension and flexion within 30 minutes. No episodes of fetal extremity extension/flexion.
Fetal movement At least three gross body movements observed within 30 minutes. Fewer than three gross body movements within 30 minutes.
Fetal breathing Sustained episode of breathing >30 s in duration, occurring at least once during a 30-min period. Absence of sustained episode of fetal breathing.
Amniotic fluid index At least one pocket of fluid >2.0 cm or an amniotic fluid index >5.0 cm. Less fluid.

💡 CLINICAL PEARL · BPP score interpretation

Scores of 8 or 10 indicates good fetal well-being and associated with normal pH. An equivocal test result (a score of 6), needs to be repeated. A score of 4 or less is predictor of abnormal outcome.

7Umbilical Artery Doppler Velocimetry

📘 KEY DEFINITION · Umbilical Artery Doppler Velocimetry

Doppler ultrasonography is a non-invasive technique to assess blood flow in the umbilical artery.

S/D ratio — most commonly used index

The umbilical artery systolic diastolic (S/D) ratio, the most commonly used index, is considered abnormal if it is above the 95th percentile for gestational age.

🚨 DANGER · Absent or reversed end-diastolic flow

Absent or reversed end-diastolic flow indicates increased resistance to umbilical artery blood flow. It is reported to result from poorly vascularized placental villi and is seen in the most extreme cases of fetal growth restriction.
