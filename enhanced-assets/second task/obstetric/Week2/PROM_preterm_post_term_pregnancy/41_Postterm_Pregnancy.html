Definition

KEY DEFINITION A pregnancy continuing beyond 42 weeks. Pregnancy between 41 to 42 weeks is called prolonged pregnancy.

MEMORY ANCHOR 41–42 = Prolonged · > 42 = Postterm. Do not confuse the two — management differs. Prolonged pregnancy is monitored; postterm pregnancy requires intervention.

Causes
Most Common Cause

Most common cause of post term pregnancy is wrong date so a careful review of menstrual history is important in all such cases.

Maternal Factors
  • Primipara.
  • History of previous prolonged pregnancy.
  • Sedentary life.
Fetal Factors
  • Anencephaly.
  • Adrenal hypoplasia (diminished fetal cortisol response).
CLINICAL PEARL Anencephaly causes postterm pregnancy because the fetal hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis is disrupted — without fetal cortisol, labor is not initiated. This is why dating must be confirmed before diagnosing postterm pregnancy.

Diagnosis — Antenatal
(A) Antenatal
  • History: calculation of gestational age.
  • Examination: larger baby size.
  • Ultrasonography:
    • Biparietal diameter more than 9.6 cm.
    • Increased fetal weight.
    • Oligohydramnios.
    • Increased placental calcification.
    • Doppler to diagnose placental insufficiency.
WARN Increased placental calcification on US is a hallmark of placental aging — it reflects the pathophysiology of postterm pregnancy where the placenta becomes insufficient.
DANGER Oligohydramnios + placental insufficiency in a postterm pregnancy = high risk for cord compression, fetal hypoxia, and meconium aspiration. Delivery should not be delayed.

Diagnosis — Postnatal
(B) Postnatal
  • Baby length: more than 54 cm.
  • Baby weight: more than 4.5 kg.
  • Skull: well ossified with smaller fontanelles.
  • Finger nails: project beyond finger tips.
MEMORY ANCHOR Postterm postnatal signs: L-W-S-F: Length > 54 cm · Weight > 4.5 kg · Skull well ossified / small fontanelles · Finger nails beyond tips.

Complications
Postterm Complications
  • Aging of placenta lead to placental insufficiency, fetal hypoxia, acidosis, and stillbirth.
  • Oligohydramnios leading to cord compression and meconium aspiration.
  • Fetal macrosomia leading to shoulder dystocia, increase the chances of birth trauma (intracranial hemorrhage) and increase the chances of operative delivery.
  • Postpartum hge and infection.
  • Increased operative delivery.
DANGER The cascade of postterm complications: placental aging → hypoxia → acidosis → stillbirth. Each day past 42 weeks increases this risk — this is why timely delivery is essential.
WARN "Postpartum hge" — source abbreviation for "hemorrhage". Macrosomic postterm babies (> 4.5 kg) carry high risk of shoulder dystocia and birth trauma.
