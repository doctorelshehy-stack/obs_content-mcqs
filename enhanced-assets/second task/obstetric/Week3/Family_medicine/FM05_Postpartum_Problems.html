Physical Problems

🩸 Postpartum Hemorrhage

Excessive bleeding after childbirth

🦠 Infections

Endometritis, UTI, wound infections (C-section or episiotomy sites)

🤱 Breastfeeding Issues

Mastitis, engorgement, cracked nipples, difficulty with latch or milk supply

🔴 Perineal Pain

Due to episiotomy or tearing during vaginal delivery

🤰 Pelvic Pain

Related to uterine involution or musculoskeletal changes

🚽 Urinary & Bowel Problems

Incontinence, constipation, hemorrhoids

😴 Fatigue & Anemia

Due to blood loss and demands of caring for a newborn

🦋 Thyroid Disorders

Postpartum thyroiditis → hyper- or hypothyroidism

💊 Contraception Needs

Counseling and initiation of appropriate birth control

Emotional & Psychological Issues

😢 Baby Blues
Mild, transient mood swings, anxiety, and irritability in first 2 weeks postpartum. Resolves on its own.
😞 Postpartum Depression (PPD)
More severe and prolonged depressive symptoms affecting mother's ability to care for herself or her baby.
😰 Postpartum Anxiety
Excessive worry, often about the baby's health and safety.
⚠️ Postpartum Psychosis
Rare but severe — hallucinations, delusions, disorganized behavior. Requires urgent psychiatric care.

Breast Engorgement

🫘 Description

Occurs when breasts become overly full, hard, and painful, usually 3–5 days postpartum when milk "comes in."
Symptoms
  • Swollen, firm, warm breasts
  • Tight, shiny skin
  • Difficulty latching for the baby
Management
  • Frequent breastfeeding or pumping
  • Hand expression to soften the areola
  • Warm compresses before feeding, cold after
  • Supportive bra without underwire
  • Pain relief: Paracetamol or Ibuprofen

Nipple Fissure (Cracked Nipples)

🔍 Description

Often due to improper latching, positioning, or excessive moisture.
Symptoms
  • Pain during and after breastfeeding
  • Visible cracks, bleeding, or scabs on the nipples
Management
  • Correct latch and positioning techniques
  • Lanolin cream or breast milk on nipples
  • Air drying nipples after feeds
  • Nipple shields in severe cases

Insufficient Milk Supply

📉 Description

When the mother feels she is not producing enough milk for the baby.
Causes
  • Poor latch
  • Infrequent feeds
  • Maternal stress
  • Dehydration
Management
  • Encourage frequent and effective breastfeeding
  • Skin-to-skin contact to stimulate milk production
  • Balanced diet and good hydration
  • Use of galactagogues

Baby Blues

DEFINITION
Baby blues refer to a mild and temporary emotional state many new mothers experience shortly after childbirth. It typically begins 2–3 days postpartum and can last up to 2 weeks. Unlike postpartum depression, baby blues are less severe and resolve on their own.

Symptoms

🎢 Mood swings — happy one moment, sad the next
😢 Crying spells — often without a clear reason
😤 Irritability — easily frustrated or annoyed
😰 Anxiety — worrying about baby's health or parenting abilities
😴 Fatigue — feeling overwhelmed and exhausted
🛌 Difficulty sleeping — even when baby is asleep
🧠 Reduced concentration — trouble focusing or making decisions
Causes
Hormonal Changes
Sudden drop in estrogen & progesterone after delivery
Physical Stress
Body's recovery from childbirth
Emotional Factors
Adjusting to new responsibilities, breastfeeding concerns, lifestyle changes
Lack of Sleep
Interrupted sleep patterns due to infant care
Management & Health Education
😴 Rest: Sleep when the baby sleeps
🤝 Accept Help: Let family/friends assist with tasks or baby care
🥗 Eat Well: Balanced diet to support energy levels
💧 Stay Hydrated: Especially important if breastfeeding
🗣️ Talk: Share feelings with partner, friend, or support group
🧘 Avoid Overwhelm: Small steps, realistic expectations
☕ Self-Care: Take short breaks to relax
🚨 WHEN TO SEEK HELP
If symptoms:
  • Last longer than 2 weeks
  • Become more intense, affecting daily functioning
  • Include feelings of hopelessness or thoughts of self-harm or harming the baby
These could indicate postpartum depression (PPD), which requires professional support from a healthcare provider.
