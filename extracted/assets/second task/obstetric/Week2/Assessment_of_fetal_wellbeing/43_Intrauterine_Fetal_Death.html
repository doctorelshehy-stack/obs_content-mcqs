  1. To understand the definition of intrauterine fetal death

2 Definition

Intrauterine Fetal Death (IUFD): It is death of the fetus in-utero after 20 weeks of pregnancy or if the fetus weight is > 500 gm.

3 Causes

Maternal

  • Diabetes (poorly controlled)
  • Systemic lupus erythematosus
  • Antiphospholipid syndrome
  • Infection
  • Hypertension
  • Preeclampsia
  • Eclampsia
  • Uterine rupture
  • Maternal trauma or death
  • Thrombophilias

Fetal

  • Intrauterine growth restriction
  • Congenital abnormality
  • Genetic abnormality
  • Infection (i.e. Parvovirus B19, CMV, Listeria)
  • Hydrops

Placental

  • Cord accident
  • Abruption
  • Premature rupture of membranes
  • Vasa previa
  • Feto-maternal hemorrhage
  • Placental insufficiency

4 Diagnosis

Symptoms

  • History of the cause.
  • Gradual disappearance of pregnancy symptoms.
  • Breast milk secretion may start.
  • Failure of abdominal enlargement.
  • Cessation of fetal movements.

Signs

  • Uterus < the period of amenorrhea.
  • Difficult palpation of fetal parts due to absent muscle tone.
  • Inaudible FHS by Doppler.

5 Investigations

  • Spalding sign: overlapping of fetal skull bones due to shrinkage of cerebrum after fetal death.
  • Robert sign: presence of gas in fetal large vessels.
  • Ball sign: hyperflexion of fetal spine.
X-ray signs recall: Spalding (skull overlap), Robert (gas), Ball (spine). SRB — scan the bones last when IUFD is suspected.

6 Complications

  • Intrauterine infection.
  • DIC and hypofibrinogenemia (after 3 weeks).
DIC time bomb: Once fetal death has persisted for > 3 weeks, the risk of maternal DIC and hypofibrinogenemia rises sharply. This is why delivery is usually offered — either to prevent DIC or, if it has already begun, to remove the antigenic stimulus. Back-to-back clotting screens plus fibrinogen are essential if expectant management is chosen.

7 Management

First principle: Termination of pregnancy should be offered after diagnosis.

1. Mode of Delivery

Mode of delivery will depend on the gestational age and obstetric history.

2. Induction of Labor

Induction of labor can be accomplished with preinduction cervical ripening followed by intravenous oxytocin.

3. Failed Induction

If failed induction, cesarean section is done.

Expectant management is conditional: Most modern protocols offer delivery within 1–2 weeks of confirmed IUFD to minimise DIC risk. Expectant management beyond 3–4 weeks without close surveillance is dangerous and rarely practiced.
