Amniotic Fluid — Dynamics & Function

Production, resorption, and physiologic role

2 panels

Amniotic fluid (AF) is accumulated in dynamic manner and its volume depends on the production and resorption of it.

Production of AF

Source shifts with gestational age

  • First half of pregnancy — from maternal circulation
  • Second half of pregnancy — fetal urination
Resorption of AF

Through fetal swallowing

Fetal swallowing is the main route of amniotic fluid clearance during the second half of pregnancy.

i
Function of AF. Amniotic fluid surrounds the growing fetus and protects the fetus from injury and temperature changes. It also allows for fetal movement and permits musculoskeletal development.

Ultrasound Assessment — SDP & AFI

Two sonographic methods to quantify amniotic fluid

2 methods · 6 cutoffs
1 — Single Deepest Pocket (SDP)

Single vertical pocket

SDP < 2 cm → Oligo SDP 2–8 cm → Normal SDP > 8 cm → Poly

SDP < 2 cm = oligohydramnios. SDP between 2 – 8 cm = Normal. SDP > 8 cm = polyhydramnios.

2 — Amniotic Fluid Index (AFI)

4-quadrant method (sum)

The deepest amniotic pocket in each of the four quadrants is measured vertically and the values added together (The 4-quadrant method).

AFI < 5 cm → Oligo AFI 10–20 cm → Normal AFI > 25 cm → Poly

AFI < 5 cm = oligohydramnios. AFI between 10 and 20 cm = normal. AFI > 25 cm = polyhydramnios.

N
No AFI "5 to <10" gray zone in the source. The source only gives three AFI states (oligo / normal / poly). Anything in between is left to clinical judgment — verify with SDP and the full clinical picture.

Complications

Maternal and fetal consequences of abnormal AF volume

Poly 6 · Oligo 3
Polyhydramnios complications
  • Maternal dyspnea
  • Preterm labor
  • Premature rupture of membranes
  • Abnormal fetal presentation
  • Umbilical cord prolapse
  • Postpartum hemorrhage
Oligohydramnios complications
  • Fetal pulmonary hypoplasia
  • Preterm labor
  • Abnormal fetal presentation
!
Shared critical complications. Both states share preterm labor and abnormal fetal presentation. In poly, also suspect umbilical cord prolapse (sudden decompression after ROM). In oligo, anticipate pulmonary hypoplasia — a major cause of neonatal mortality when oligohydramnios is prolonged.

Management

Cause-specific and volume-directed interventions

Poly 3 · Oligo 3
Polyhydramnios management
  1. Amnio-reduction
  2. Prostaglandin synthetase inhibitor
  3. Treatment of the cause e.g. DM
Oligohydramnios management
  1. Amnio-infusion
  2. Rehydration of the mother
  3. Post-term pregnancy: termination
★
Always treat the cause. Source places "Treatment of the cause" (e.g. DM) under poly management, but the same principle applies to oligohydramnios (treat PROM, renal agenesis, post-term pregnancy, placental insufficiency as appropriate).
⚠
Post-term + oligohydramnios = deliver. The source lists "Postterm pregnancy: termination" as an oligo management step. When the pregnancy is post-term and AF is low, the next move is delivery rather than watchful waiting.
