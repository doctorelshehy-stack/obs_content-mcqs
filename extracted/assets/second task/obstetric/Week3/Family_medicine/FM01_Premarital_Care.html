Overview

Marriage is the fundamental building block of society, and the cornerstone in building emotional, healthy and family relationships. The healthy marriage guarantees protecting the family members from the hereditary and infectious diseases; thus building a happy and stable family.

DEFINITION
Premarital care is the specific service provided for the couples before marriage. By caring for the health of present children and adolescents, we are actually starting to provide care for the future generation.
TIMING
Couples planning to marry are advised to conduct premarital screening at least three months before the marriage date. The premarital compatibility certificate is valid for only six months.
3

Components of Premarital Care

① Premarital Assessment
Screening: History, examination, lab investigations
② Premarital Counselling
General, reproductive, genetic counselling
③ Immunization
Rubella vaccination for females
Objectives
  • Promote health of future parents
  • Prevent health hazards
  • Have healthy future generations
Consanguinity Note
Among offspring of consanguineous marriage, there is increased prenatal morbidity and mortality together with increased incidence of congenital abnormalities and mental retardation.
Inter-conception Care
Caring for females in-between pregnancies

Premarital Assessment (Premarital Screening)

A. Record Personal Information
  • Name, national ID number, age, education, occupation, contact number, and consanguinity.
B. History Taking
Chronic Diseases
DM, thyroid, HTN, cardiac or any psychological troubles
Infections & Transfusions
Rubella (females), Mumps (males), Blood transfusion history
Genetic Disorders
SCD, Thalassemia, G6PD — Previous children with congenital abnormality
Family History
SCD, Thalassemia, G6PD, Congenital anomalies (Marfan, deaf mutism)
C. Clinical Examination
Vital signs: BP, pulse, RR
Systemic: Pallor, jaundice, thyroid, chest, CVS
Extra examination according to case
D. Laboratory Investigations
Routine Obligatory
  • CBC
  • Blood group (ABO & Rh)
  • HPLC (Hb variants)
  • Sickledex
  • G6PD
  • Ferritin
  • HBsAg, HCV
  • Infertility tests
Optional
Based on findings of examination

Premarital Counselling

DEFINITION
Counselling is an interpersonal communication designed to help another person to make a rationale decision. Premarital counseling has been defined as a process of helping couple learn how to make wise and realistic decisions, through providing information and altering their own behaviors to produce desirable consequences.

Process of Counselling:

👂 Listening — be accepting, concerned, patient, specific, honest and faithful
💬 Discussion
🗣️ Talking & Negotiation — Explaining & Education
Types of Premarital Counselling
I — General Counselling & Health Education
Promoting healthy lifestyle, protection against preventable disease and unhealthy behaviors such as smoking, addiction.
II — Reproductive Health Counselling
Advice about appropriate time of 1st pregnancy, importance of early antenatal care, precautions during pregnancy (avoid drugs & teratogenic hazards), safe delivery.
III — Relevant Health Problem Counselling
Management of cases and referral to specialists for any discovered problem during premarital assessment.
IV — Genetic Counselling
Studying genogram of both parents to discover probability of genetic hazards to offspring (SCD, thalassemia, Marfan syndrome, deaf mutism). Referral for genetic testing when indicated.
7

Immunization

RECOMMENDATION
Encourage immunization of females against rubella, not less than 3 months before planning pregnancy to avoid congenital rubella syndrome if the female acquires the infection during pregnancy.
8

Inter-conception Care

DEFINITION
Caring for females in-between pregnancies aimed to:
  1. Promote the health and nutritional status of the mother
  2. Identify risks both for the mother and the future children and deal with them
  3. Decrease maternal morbidity and mortality
  4. Decrease neonatal morbidity and mortality

When can inter-conception care be provided?

During Post partum / post abortive care
When attending the MCH for her child care
During home visits
Components of Inter-conception Care
I — Counselling
  1. Contraceptive counselling — help females choose most appropriate method
  2. Nutritional counselling
  3. Genetic counselling — when history of recurrent abortion, cervical incompetence, congenital abnormalities
II — Health Promotion
General health education and promotion
III — Risk Assessment & Modification
  • Recently diagnosed maternal diseases (DM, HTN)
  • Obstetric history: previous complicated pregnancy, bad outcome (Down, β-thalassaemia, SCD, NTD, congenital hypothyroidism)
  • Conditions in the newborn/child
