Definition & Goal of Antenatal Care

DEFINITION
Antenatal care (ANC) is an umbrella term used to describe the medical procedures and care that are carried out during pregnancy. It is the care a woman receives throughout her pregnancy and is important in helping to ensure a healthy pregnancy state and safe childbirth.

Goal of Antenatal Care

🎯 Ultimate Goal
Reduce maternal and perinatal mortality and morbidity rates.
Prepare the woman for labor, lactation, and care of her infant. Detect early and treat properly complicated conditions that could endanger the life or impair the health of the mother or the fetus.
How This is Achieved
  1. Detection & management of complications (medical, surgical, obstetrical)
  2. Promote physical, mental, social health of mother & baby — nutrition, hygiene, birthing process
  3. Develop birth preparedness & complication readiness plan
  4. Prepare mother to breastfeed successfully, experience normal puerperium, and care for child
3

Components of Antenatal Care

1. Registration & detailed history taking
2. Periodic examination & lab investigation
3. Detection & management of high-risk pregnancy
4. Health education
5. Nutritional care
6. Emotional & social care
7. Preventive measures (tetanus toxoid immunization)
8. Referral when needed

Timing & Visit Schedule

FIRST VISIT
Ideally the pregnant lady should seek antenatal care as early as possible during the first trimester.
Standard Visit Frequency
  • Every 4 weeks till the 28th week of gestation
  • Every 2 weeks from 28th to 36th week of gestation
  • Every week from 36th week till labor
Minimum Required 8 Visits — Timeline
1
Visit 1
~First Trimester
First antenatal visit — as early as possible in the first trimester. History, exam, baseline labs, dating US.
2
Visit 2
~20-26 wks
Fetal anatomic screening US (18–20 wks). Triple/quad screen (15–20 wks).
3
Visit 3
~20-26 wks
Second visit in 20–26 week window. GDM screening (24–28 wks by 50g GCT).
4
Visit 4
~30-34 wks
BP check, fundal height, fetal presentation assessment. Urine analysis.
5
Visit 5
~30-34 wks
Second visit in 30–34 week window. Continue monitoring growth & BP.
6
Visit 6
~36-38 wks
Leopold maneuvers for presentation (from 36 wks). Birth preparedness counselling.
7
Visit 7
~36-38 wks
Second visit in 36–38 week window. Re-check presentation, discuss labor signs.
8
Visit 8
~40 wks
Final visit at 40 weeks. Confirm fetal well-being, plan for delivery.

Barriers to Effective Antenatal Care

1. Poor access
Poor access to medical services
2. Poverty
Financial constraints limiting healthcare utilization
3. Cultural norms
Women needing spouses' consent before receiving medical care
4. Quality perception
Prolonged outpatient waiting time, unprofessional conduct of service providers
5. Inadequate facilities
Lack of emergency obstetric care due to inadequate facilities

First Antenatal Care Visit

A. History Taking
1. LMP & EDD
Date of last menstrual period to calculate expected date of delivery by Naegele's rule (subtract 3 months, add 7 days to 1st day of LMP). For irregular cycles, EDD by US before 24 weeks.
2. Current Problems
Nausea & vomiting, abdominal pain, headache, urinary complaints, vaginal bleeding, edema, backache, heartburn, constipation.
3. Past Medical & Surgical
Chronic diseases (DM, HTN, renal), prior operations (CS, genital repair), blood transfusion, accidents involving bony pelvis, allergies, medications.
4. Gynecological History
Menstrual history, contraceptive history, history of STDs.
5-6. Medications & Nutrition
Current prescriptions & OTC, substance use (alcohol, tobacco), nutritional history.
7-8. Family & Obstetric History
Family: DM, HTN, heart disease, multiple pregnancy, hereditary diseases.
Obstetric: gravida, para, abortion, living children, previous complications, type of delivery, birth weights.
9-10. Genetic & Social History
Genetic: hereditary diseases.
Social: Was pregnancy planned/unwanted? Screen for intimate partner violence — increases risk of spontaneous abortion, placental abruption, PROM, LBW, prematurity.
B. Physical Examination
General Examination
  • Blood pressure (baseline)
  • Thyroid, breast exam
  • Height & body weight (baseline for BMI)
  • Edema evaluation
Local Examination
  • Fundal level assessment (after 12 wks)
  • Auscultation fetal heart tones: Doppler 10-12 wks, Pinard's stethoscope after 20 wks
Ultrasound
  • Estimate gestational age
  • Check amniotic fluid volume
  • Placental position
  • Detect multiple pregnancy & congenital malformations
  • Fetal position
Dating accuracy: ±4-7 days (1st tri), ±10-14 days (2nd tri), ±21 days (3rd tri).
Standard: offer single US at 18-20 wks for anatomic screening.
📋 Routine Lab
Urine analysis · ABO grouping & Rh typing · Complete blood count · Pelvic US
➕ Other Tests
HBsAg · Urine culture · Pap smear · Cervical swab (gonorrhea, chlamydia) · Toxoplasmosis IgG, IgM

Repeated ANC Visits

Each subsequent visit includes the following components (a–j):

a) Update History
Update the patient's history for any new complaints.
b) Evaluate Symptoms
Evaluate symptoms suggestive of complications — gestational hypertension, infections (urinary tract, vaginal), placenta previa: headache, blurring of vision, vaginal bleeding, abdominal pain, dysuria, vaginal discharge, and subjective sensation of fetal movement.
c) Assess Weight Gain
At 20th week ~3.5 kg gained, then ~0.5 kg/week. Total: 11–16 kg.
⚠ Excessive gain → occult edema of pre-eclampsia
⚠ Failure to gain → fetal jeopardy
d) Assess Blood Pressure
BP measurement at each visit to detect pregnancy-induced hypertension.
e) Assess Fundal Height
Measurement of fundal height is recommended at each prenatal visit beginning at 20 weeks.
f) Auscultate Fetal Heart Rate
Recommended at each prenatal visit to confirm a viable fetus.
g) Evaluate Edema
Evaluate edema of the extremities or face — may indicate pregnancy-induced hypertension.
h) Leopold Maneuvers
Abdominal palpation to assess fetal presentation beginning at 36 weeks' gestation (less accurate earlier in pregnancy).
Investigations at Each Visit
i) Urine Analysis
Recommended at each visit for protein, glucose, or infection.
j) Screening Tests During Pregnancy
  1. Trisomy & NTD screening (15–20 wks): Triple screen (HCG, unconjugated estriol, α-fetoprotein) or quadruple screen (+ inhibin-A). Nuchal translucency by US.
  2. GDM screening (24–28 wks): 1-hour 50g glucose challenge test. Value >140 mg/dl → abnormal. Positive → 3-hour GTT (100g glucose, fasting + 1h + 2h + 3h). Two of four positive = gestational diabetes.

Weight Gain Guidelines During Pregnancy

RECOMMENDATION
Recommendations for total weight gain during pregnancy, by pre-pregnancy or early pregnancy (less than 10 weeks) BMI
Pre-pregnancy BMI (kg/m²) Total Weight Gain Range
Underweight (< 18.5) 12.5 kg – 18 kg
Healthy weight (18.5 – 24.9) 11.5 kg – 16 kg
Overweight (25.0 – 29.9) 7 kg – 11.5 kg
Obese (≥ 30.0) 5 kg – 9 kg

Detection & Management of High-Risk Pregnancy

IMPORTANT
Risky pregnancy can be detected from history and first antenatal examination or may develop during pregnancy. Early detection is important to prevent complications and hazards.

I — Risk Factors Detected from History

Mother's age <18 or >35 years
Primigravida or grand multipara (5th pregnancy)
Birth spacing <2 years
Previous fetal death / repeated abortions
History of Caesarean section
Previous baby with congenital anomalies
Chronic diseases (HTN, DM, renal, heart)

II — Risk Factors Detected Early in Pregnancy

Diabetes mellitus
Severe anemia
Severe persistent vomiting
Severe undernutrition

III — Risk Factors Detected Late in Pregnancy

Severe PIH
Antepartum hemorrhage
Multiple pregnancy
IUGR
Abnormal presentation
MANAGEMENT
High risk pregnancy should be observed more closely and referred to specialist when needed. Mandatory hospital delivery.

Immunization During Pregnancy

💉 Tetanus Toxoid (TT)
Recommended for pregnant women.
  • Two doses with 4 weeks apart
  • Subsequent pregnancy: one dose
💉 COVID-19 Vaccines
Strongly recommended in pregnancy. Best protection against known risks including ICU admission for the woman and premature birth.

Health Education During ANC

1. Importance of ANC
Recommended dates and date of the next visit
2. Personal Hygiene
  • Daily all-over wash, warm shower/sponge bath (not tub)
  • Avoid hot baths (fatigue & fainting)
  • Regular washing for genital area, axilla, breast
  • Avoid vaginal douches except if excessive secretion/infection
  • Avoid tight clothes, belts, constricting bands on legs
  • Low heel shoes, loose light clothes
3. Exercise
  • At least 30 min moderate exercise (walking ideal)
  • Avoid falls, abdominal injuries, heavy weights
  • Avoid long periods of standing (varicose veins)
4. Sleep
  • At least 8 hours sleep every night
  • Lie down to relax/sleep 1-2 hours afternoon
  • Best position: Sims' position
5-7. Dental & Substance Avoidance
  • Regular dental examination & cleaning
  • Avoid any drugs unless proven safe (especially 1st trimester)
  • Avoid alcohol & tobacco
⚠ Smoking increases risk: preterm birth, LBW, cleft lip/palate, SIDS
8. Air Travel
Safe up to 4 weeks before due date. Long flights → increased risk of venous thrombosis.
9-10. Nutrition & Workplace
  • Nutritional counselling (discussed separately)
  • Avoid prolonged standing, hard work, chemical exposure
12-13. Special Counselling
  • Age >35: counsel about Down syndrome risk & testing options
  • Breastfeeding: recommend as best feeding method
14-16. Labor & Beyond
  • Counsel about labor signs, pain management, support during labor
  • Essentials of baby care
  • Contraception counselling for birth spacing

Dangerous Signs During Pregnancy (Red Flags)

🚨 SEEK IMMEDIATE CARE
If any of the following develop during pregnancy, the woman should seek immediate medical care.
🩸 Vaginal bleeding including spotting
🤰 Persistent abdominal pain
🤕 Persistent headache
👁️ Blurring of vision
💧 Leakage of amniotic fluid
🌡️ Fever
👶 Stoppage of fetal movement for >4 hours in late pregnancy
