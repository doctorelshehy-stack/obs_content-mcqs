## Question 1
**c) Middle layer of myometrium** — The middle (vascular/net-like) layer of the myometrium acts as the "living ligature" that constricts uterine vessels during the third stage of labour.

## Question 2
**c) Atosiban** — Atosiban is a tocolytic (oxytocin receptor antagonist) used to suppress preterm labour, not to cause abortion. Oxytocin and prostaglandins are abortifacients.

## Question 3
**c) Aneuploidy** — Chromosomal abnormalities (aneuploidy) account for ~50–60% of spontaneous first-trimester losses. Cervical incompetence typically causes second-trimester loss.

## Question 4
**a) Abortion occurs in first trimester** — Cervical incompetence characteristically causes recurrent mid-trimester (second-trimester) abortions/pregnancy loss, not first-trimester.

## Question 5
**a) Eisenmenger syndrome** — Eisenmenger syndrome carries the highest maternal mortality in pregnancy (30–50%) due to fixed pulmonary hypertension and right-to-left shunt.

## Question 6
**d) Rh incompatibility** — Rh incompatibility causes immune hydrops fetalis (anti-D alloimmunisation). The others (parvovirus, Down's, alpha-thal, TTTS) cause non-immune hydrops.

## Question 7
**e) Uterine contraction** — The Bishop score includes cervical dilatation, effacement, station, position, and consistency. Uterine contractions are not a component.

## Question 8
**d) Severe internal bleeding** — Shoulder pain (Kehr's sign) in ectopic pregnancy indicates diaphragmatic irritation from intra-abdominal blood, signifying rupture with significant haemorrhage.

## Question 9
**b) Gestational diabetes** — Molar pregnancy is associated with early-onset pre-eclampsia/PIH, thyrotoxicosis (due to high hCG), hyperemesis, and theca lutein cysts, but not GDM.

## Question 10
**a) Theca lutein cysts** — High hCG levels in hydatidiform mole cause bilateral theca lutein (luteal) ovarian cysts, which regress after molar evacuation.

## Question 11
**a) Submento-bregmatic** — At 9.5 cm, the submento-bregmatic diameter is the smallest of the listed skull diameters; it presents in full extension (face presentation).

## Question 12
**c) Serum ferritin** — Serum ferritin is the earliest and most sensitive laboratory marker for iron depletion; it reflects body iron stores before anaemia develops.

## Question 13
**a) E. coli** — *Escherichia coli* is the most common causative organism of acute pyelonephritis in pregnancy.

## Question 14
**a) Mild cephalopelvic disproportion** — Trial of labour may be attempted in mild CPD. Severe CPD, shoulder presentation, and certain malpresentations are contraindications.

## Question 15
**a) Acute uterine inversion** — Postpartum protrusion of a mass from the vagina following delivery is the classic presentation of acute uterine inversion.

## Question 16
**a) Event of labour** — The partogram graphically records the progress of labour, including cervical dilatation and fetal descent, to detect abnormal labour.

## Question 17
**d) Hepatitis E** — Hepatitis E has the highest maternal mortality rate (up to 20–25%) in pregnancy, especially in the third trimester, with risk of fulminant hepatitis.

## Question 18
**a) Placenta previa centralis** — Central/complete placenta previa completely covers the cervical os, precluding vaginal delivery; caesarean section is mandatory.

## Question 19
**b) Phosphatidylglycerol** — Phosphatidylglycerol (PG) is not affected by blood or meconium contamination in amniotic fluid, unlike L/S ratio, bilirubin, and AFP.

## Question 20
**a) Mento-anterior** — Face-to-pubis delivery is possible in mento-anterior (face presentation with chin anterior), not in OP position which typically requires rotation.

## Question 21
**b) Abdominal pain** — The most constant symptom of undisturbed ectopic pregnancy is abdominal/pelvic pain (present in ~95%), while amenorrhea may be absent or unnoticed.

## Question 22
**d) Maternal urine** — Maternal urine does not contain fetal cells or DNA suitable for genetic prenatal diagnosis; fetal blood, amniotic fluid, and CVS are appropriate.

## Question 23
**c) Protect against ovarian cysts** — COCs suppress ovulation, reducing the risk of functional ovarian cysts. They also reduce iron-deficiency anaemia (lighter menses) and endometrial cancer risk.

## Question 24
**b) Apply cold compresses after feeding** — Cold compresses after feeding reduce vasodilation and engorgement. Warmth before feeding helps let-down; cold after feeding reduces swelling.

## Question 25
**a) As early as possible** — The first antenatal care visit should occur as soon as pregnancy is suspected (ideally within the first trimester) for early risk assessment.

## Question 48
**b) Elevated serum cholesterol level** — Elevated cholesterol alone is not a standard risk factor for high-risk pregnancy classification. Multiple pregnancy, domestic violence, prior macrosomic baby, and IUGR are recognised risk factors.

## Question 49

## Question 50

## Question 51
**a) Vegetables** — The food guide pyramid's second layer (from bottom) comprises vegetables and fruits. Grains form the base; dairy and meats are above; fats/oils/sweets are the tip.

## Question 52
**e) Cholelithiasis** — COCs increase the risk of gallstones/cholelithiasis (due to altered bile composition) and do not improve it. COCs improve iron-deficiency anaemia, dysmenorrhea, acne, and reduce ectopic pregnancy risk.

## Question 53
**d) Promotion for breastfeeding** — At 10 weeks, the focus is on early pregnancy topics (nutrition, hygiene, danger signs). Breastfeeding promotion is typically emphasised in the late second/third trimester.

## Question 54
**a) 10–14 days** — When a fetal anomaly is suspected on ultrasound, follow-up scans are typically repeated every 10–14 days to assess evolution.

## Question 55
**a) Inevitable abortion** — At 20 weeks with a dilated (open) cervical os and bleeding, but no products expelled, the diagnosis is inevitable abortion (the process has started and cannot be halted).

## Question 56
**d) Oxytocin challenge test** — The biophysical profile (BPP) includes: NST, fetal breathing movements, fetal body movements, fetal tone, and amniotic fluid volume. The OCT is not a component.

## Question 57
**b) Convulsions** — Eclampsia is defined by the new onset of tonic-clonic seizures in a woman with preeclampsia. The presence of convulsions differentiates eclampsia from preeclampsia.

## Question 58
**c) 300** — The standard postpartum dose of anti-D immunoglobulin for Rh-negative mothers with an Rh-positive baby is 300 mcg (1500 IU).

## Question 59
**a) Mento-vertical diameter** — In brow presentation (partially deflexed head), the engaging diameter is the mento-vertical (13.5 cm), the largest fetal skull diameter, making vaginal delivery usually impossible.

## Question 60
**a) Diabetes mellitus** — Caudal regression syndrome (sacral agenesis) is a congenital anomaly strongly associated with pregestational diabetes, thought to result from hyperglycaemia-induced disruption.

## Question 61
**b) Decreased fibrinogen** — During pregnancy, fibrinogen (factor I) increases by ~50% as part of the hypercoagulable state. It does not decrease. The other options are normal physiological changes.

## Question 62
**b) In fundal grip head felts with difficulty** — In complete breech, the head occupies the fundus but is felt with difficulty through the fundal grip because it is deeply engaged in the fundal cavity.

## Question 63
**e) Bilateral renal agenesis** — Bilateral renal agenesis causes severe oligohydramnios (Potter sequence), not polyhydramnios. Twinning, anencephaly, oesophageal atresia, and maternal diabetes are all associated with polyhydramnios.

## Question 64
**a) March 17** — Naegele's rule: LMP June 10 + 7 days = June 17, − 3 months = March 17. For a 32-day cycle adjustment, the difference from 28 days is +4 days → March 21, but the standard Naegele rule gives March 17.

## Question 65
**b) Variable deceleration** — Variable decelerations are abrupt decreases in FHR caused by umbilical cord compression; they typically last <2 minutes and vary in timing/shape relative to contractions.

## Question 66
**e) Suction evacuation** — Theca lutein cysts associated with hydatidiform mole regress spontaneously after the mole is evacuated by suction evacuation. No direct ovarian surgery is needed.

## Question 67
**c) 50-gram glucose challenge test** — The 50-g GCT is the most sensitive screening test for GDM (though not diagnostic). The formal diagnostic test is the 75-g or 100-g OGTT.

## Question 68
**c) Thalassemia** — Microcytic anaemia with normal serum ferritin (normal iron stores) points to thalassemia trait, not iron deficiency. Folate/B12 deficiencies cause macrocytic anaemia.

## Question 69
**a) Prophylactic forceps** — Prophylactic (outlet) forceps delivery shortens the second stage, reducing maternal pushing effort and cardiac work in women with heart disease.

## Question 70
**b) Anterior division of internal iliac artery** — Ligation of the anterior division of the internal iliac artery (hypogastric artery) reduces pelvic pulse pressure by ~50% and can control postpartum haemorrhage.

## Question 71
**d) Obstetric conjugate** — The obstetric conjugate (from sacral promontory to the nearest point on the posterior pubic symphysis, ~10 cm) is the smallest AP diameter of the pelvic inlet.

## Question 72
**b) Attitude of the fetus** — Attitude (or habitus) is the relationship of fetal body parts to one another (degree of flexion/extension). Lie is the relationship of the fetal axis to the maternal axis; presentation is the presenting part.

## Question 73
**e) Carboprost** — Carboprost (15-methyl PGF2α, Hemabate) is the most potent uterotonic among the listed agents for atonic PPH, though oxytocin is first-line for initial management.

## Question 74
**a) Restitution** — After the head delivers, it rotates ⅛ of a circle to align with the fetal shoulders. This external rotation is called restitution.

## Question 75
**a) Anencephaly** — Periconceptional folic acid supplementation reduces the risk of neural tube defects including anencephaly and spina bifida.

## Question 76
**a) 2nd twin** — Internal podalic version is most commonly performed for the second twin in a transverse or malpositioned presentation after vaginal delivery of the first twin.

## Question 77
**d) The onset of labour** — Prelabour rupture of membranes (PROM) is defined as rupture of the amniotic membranes before the onset of labour, regardless of gestational age.

## Question 78
**c) Relieved by enema** — False labour pains (Braxton Hicks) are irregular, not associated with cervical dilation, and may be relieved by enema, hydration, or rest. True labour is regular, progressive, and causes cervical change.

## Question 79
**e) Can detect fetal congenital anomalies early** — At 10 weeks, detailed fetal anatomy cannot yet be reliably assessed. The anomaly scan is performed at 18–22 weeks. Dating, viability, and multiplicity can be assessed in the first trimester.

## Question 80
**d) Insert the IUD today** — Immediate postpartum IUD insertion (within 10 minutes of placental delivery) is safe and effective, reducing the risk of loss to follow-up.

## Question 81
**d) Constipation is common with iron supplements** — Iron supplements commonly cause constipation (and other GI side effects). Vitamin C enhances absorption; milk/coffee/tea inhibit it.

## Question 82
**e) Counseling about healthy eating and keeping physically active during pregnancy is recommended** — For a normal-BMI woman (Hb 11.5 g/dL, not anaemic), the priority health message is healthy eating and physical activity, not iron supplementation.

## Question 83
**d) Oral glucose tolerance** — OGTT is performed at 24–28 weeks routinely, not at the booking visit. CBC, blood type, and Rh are standard first-trimester investigations.

## Question 84
**d) Between days 4 and 6 of the menses** — The optimal timing for IUD insertion is during menstruation (days 4–6) to ensure the woman is not pregnant and the cervix is slightly open.

## Question 85
**a) There is history of recurrent abortion** — Recurrent pregnancy loss is a strong indication for pre-conception genetic testing (karyotyping of both partners) to identify balanced translocations.

## Question 89

## Question 90

## Question 91
